The Enigma was an electromechanical rotor cipher machine used by Nazi Germany to encrypt military communications.

The machine was a combination of **mechanical and electrical** subsystems, using a complex wiring path that changed with every key press, creating a polyalphabetic substitution cipher.

1. Keyboard & Lampboard

When a key (e.g., 'A') is pressed, a complex electrical circuit is activated. The resulting encrypted letter lights up on the **Lampboard** (e.g., 'Z'). The machine is *reciprocal*‚Äîtyping 'A' yields 'Z', and typing 'Z' yields 'A' with the same settings.

2. Plugboard (Steckerbrett)

The first and last stage of scrambling. Cables are used to swap pairs of letters (e.g., A is swapped with L, P with R). This adds a crucial layer of initial and final substitution, vastly increasing the number of potential keys.

3. Rotors (Walzen)

Typically **three** (but later four) interchangeable rotors, each with 26 electrical contacts and a unique internal wiring pattern. They are mounted on a spindle, and the current flows through them in series. Crucially, the rightmost rotor **steps (rotates)** one position with every key press, changing the entire wiring path for the next letter.

4. Reflector (Umkehrwalze)

A fixed wired disk that receives the current after it passes through the rotors, then sends it back through the rotors via a different path. This is what makes the encryption reciprocal, but also creates the **critical cryptographic flaw**: a letter can never be encrypted as itself.

Cracking the Enigma code, a top-secret project codenamed **ULTRA** by the Allies, is widely considered one of the single most important factors that shortened the war.

The Codebreakers

  • **Polish Cipher Bureau:** First successfully reverse-engineered the Enigma's internal wiring in the early 1930s (led by **Marian Rejewski**). They passed their vital work to the British just before the war.
  • **Bletchley Park, UK:** The main hub for Allied code-breaking. Mathematician **Alan Turing** led the team that designed the **'Bombe'**‚Äîan electromechanical machine used to rapidly deduce the daily Enigma settings.

Impact on the War

  • **Battle of the Atlantic:** Decrypted U-boat messages helped Allied convoys evade submarine "wolf packs," securing vital supply lines.
  • **Strategic Advantage:** Ultra intelligence gave Allied commanders advance notice of German military plans, troop movements, and attack intentions across all fronts, including North Africa and D-Day.
  • **Shortening the War:** The ability to read German secrets is estimated to have shortened the war by as much as **two years**, saving countless lives.
